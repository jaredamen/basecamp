QUESTION
Why does an ambulance siren sound higher-pitched as it approaches and lower as it drives away?
Tap card to reveal answer
ANSWER
It's the Doppler effect — the observed frequency of a wave shifts when the source and observer move relative to each other.
Picture wavefronts as ripples in a pond. The siren racing toward you bunches its ripples in front (higher pitch), and stretches them out behind (lower pitch). Same waves — the motion squeezes time itself.
