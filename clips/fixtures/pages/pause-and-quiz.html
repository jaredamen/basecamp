Stoicism

Stoicism is a school of Hellenistic philosophy founded by Zeno of Citium in Athens in the early 3rd century BC. It is a philosophy of personal virtue ethics, informed by its system of logic and its views on the natural world.

According to Stoicism, the path to eudaimonia (happiness, or blessedness) is found in accepting the moment as it presents itself, by not allowing oneself to be controlled by the desire for pleasure or by the fear of pain.

The Stoics maintained that virtue is the only good for human beings, and that external things, such as health, wealth, and pleasure, are not good or bad in themselves but rather have value as "material for virtue to act upon."
